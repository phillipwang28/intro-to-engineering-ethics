What is Engineering Ethics?

Engineering ethics is the study of the moral principles that guide the practice of engineering. It ensures public safety, honesty, and fairness in design and implementation. With the power to revolutionize society right at our fingertips, engineers have much control over how society progresses, so it is essential for us to have a dynamic understanding of ethics as technology progresses.

Core Principles

Explore the foundational principles of engineering ethics and think about how you would define each principle:

  • Public Safety: Ensuring the safety and welfare of the public in engineering decisions.
  • Honesty: Maintaining transparency and truthfulness in design and communication without withholding harmful information.
  • Sustainability: Promoting environmentally responsible practices.
  • Equity: Treating all stakeholders fairly and without discrimination, and promoting a diverse and inclusive work environment.
